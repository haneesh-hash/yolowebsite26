Signature Experience

Ranglung Gompa & Sacred Peaks

The experience that is uniquely YOLO Outdoors — a monastery walk that begins at your doorstep.

Duration 3–4 Hours
Difficulty Easy
Season Year-round
Altitude 3,200m

Overview

Walk from Your Dome to the Monastery

This is the experience that defines YOLO Outdoors. A short walk — no vehicle needed — from your geodesic dome along the Bhaga River to Ranglung Gompa, one of the oldest Buddhist monasteries in the Lahaul valley. The gompa sits quietly above the river, prayer flags streaming in the wind, with a meditation viewpoint facing a sacred unnamed peak that the local community holds as a guardian mountain. No guide is required, but we can arrange a local host to share stories of the valley and the monastery’s history.

The Walk

Step by Step

Step 1 Leave Your Dome +

Walk out the door. The trail starts from the YOLO Outdoors property itself. Cross the Bhaga River bridge and turn uphill toward the village. The air at 3,200m is clean, thin, and sharp.

Step 2 River Trail & Village Path +

A gentle 20–30 minute walk along the riverbank and through Jispa village. Pass traditional Lahauli stone houses, barley fields, and apricot trees. The path is flat and easy — suitable for all fitness levels.

Step 3 Ranglung Gompa +

The monastery sits above the village with the entire valley spread below. Prayer wheels, ancient murals, and the quiet hum of centuries. Sit in the courtyard. Watch the prayer flags. Listen to the wind.

Step 4 Sacred Peak Viewpoint +

A short climb above the gompa to a meditation viewpoint. The sacred unnamed peak fills your entire field of vision — snow-capped, immovable. Locals consider it a guardian of the valley. This is the moment you came for.

Step 5 Return to Your Dome +

Walk back at your own pace. The return is downhill and takes about 20 minutes. Arrive at your dome. Tea on the deck. The mountain is still there.
